A Glimpse over time


There have been various studies and research about this certain topic
in the timeline of many years. Indeed, it is a crucial topic to study as it
involves the evolution of mankind from each generation to have ever lived. According to
The National Institutes of Health, many theories claim that fertility rate is inversely
proportional to lifespan(or vice versa); Increased lifespan leads to a reduced fertility.
Interestingly enough, there had been some studies of organisms that supported those
multiple theories, with the exception of HUMANS; Strange, it was...HOWEVER, only in the
case of historical populations. As for contemporary populations, the inverse proportion
was seen. Researchers suspected that it was due to genes.
Now, here come a few of the theories involved in this research:

The antagonistic pleiotropy theory and the disposable soma theory.
Simply put, the antagonistic pleiotropy theory tells us that an increase in early reproductive
life would result to an increased risk of diseases and mortality later on. The disposable soma
theory is quite similar, except in focuses on how reproduction comes with energetic and
metabolic costs which may lead to more serious conditions later on.
